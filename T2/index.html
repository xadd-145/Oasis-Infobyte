Biography

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam often referred to as the "People's President," was an eminent Indian scientist and statesman. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, India, he played a pivotal role in the nation's space and missile development programs, particularly the success of India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests. He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and was known for his vision of making India a developed nation by 2020. Dr. Kalam was a prominent advocate for education, particularly in science and technology, and authored several books. He passed away on July 27, 2015, leaving behind a lasting legacy of scientific achievement, leadership, and inspiration for generations to come.

Early Life

In his school years, Kalam had average grades but was described as a bright and hardworking student who had a strong desire to learn. He spent hours on his studies, especially Mathematics. After completing his education at the Schwartz Higher Secondary School, Ramanathapuram, Kalam went on to attend Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, then affiliated with the University of Madras, from where he graduated in physics in 1954. He moved to Madras in 1955 to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology.

Career as Scientist

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, a renowned Indian scientist, made significant contributions to aerospace engineering and missile technology during his career. He played a crucial role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) and was a key figure in India's ballistic missile program. His leadership and dedication to scientific research, coupled with his commitment to educating and inspiring young minds, left an enduring legacy in India's scientific and technological advancements.

Life as President

During his tenure as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007, Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam brought a unique blend of humility and visionary leadership to the position. He was affectionately known as the "People's President" and used his role to inspire the youth and promote education and scientific research. Dr. Kalam's presidency was marked by his efforts to connect with the citizens of India, especially the younger generation, and he continued to champion his vision of a developed India. His presidency remains an exemplary period of public service dedicated to the betterment of the nation.

Greatest achievements

  • 1969

    Rohini Satellite

    Kalam played a significant role in India's first satellite launch, Rohini Satellite, while working at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

  • 1980s

    Missile Development Program

    He led India's Integrated Guided Missile Development Program, which resulted in the successful development of five missile systems: Prithvi, Trishul, Akash, Nag, and Agni. This contributed to India's self-reliance in defense technology.

  • 1998

    Nuclear tests at Pokhran

    Kalam played a crucial role in India's successful nuclear tests in Pokhran, codenamed Operation Shakti. These tests established India as a nuclear weapons state.

  • 2002-2007

    President of India

    Dr. Kalam served as the 11th President of India, making him the first scientist to hold this prestigious office. His presidency was marked by his focus on education, youth, and technology.

  • Books

    Books: Throughout his life, Dr. Kalam authored numerous books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," which continue to inspire readers, especially the youth.

  • Vision 2020

    He advocated for and laid out a vision for India to become a developed nation by 2020, emphasizing technology, education, and economic growth.

  • Missile Defense Systems

    Under his leadership, India developed its Ballistic Missile Defense (BMD) system, aimed at providing protection against incoming enemy missiles.

  • Abdul Kalam Island

    India's Wheeler Island, used for missile testing, was renamed Abdul Kalam Island in his honor.
